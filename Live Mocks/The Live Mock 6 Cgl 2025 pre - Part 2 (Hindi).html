Solution
The “Law of Triads” given by J W Dobereiner, stating that when elements are arranged in increasing order of atomic mass, groups of three elements [called triads] can be formed, where the atomic mass of the middle element is roughly equal to the average of the atomic masses of the other two elements in the triad, and these elements also share similar chemical properties; essentially, it was an early attempt to classify elements based on their atomic masses and recurring patterns in their properties.
