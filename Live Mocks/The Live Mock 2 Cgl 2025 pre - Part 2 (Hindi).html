    Five-kingdom classification was proposed by__?

        Whittaker

        Huxley

        Linnaeus

        Lamarck

    Solution
    In 1969, Whittaker classified all organisms into five major kingdoms based on their structure, mode of nutrition, and other biological characteristics. This classification divided organisms into the following five kingdoms:

  • Monera:

    • This kingdom includes all unicellular microorganisms such as bacteria and archaea.

    • These organisms are prokaryotic (without a nucleus) and are microscopic in size.

  • Protista:

    • This kingdom consists of unicellular organisms, such as amoeba, paramecium, and some algae.

    • These organisms are unicellular but have a nucleus (eukaryotic).

  • Plantae:

    • This kingdom includes all plants, which are multicellular and make their food through photosynthesis.

    • These organisms are eukaryotic and are primarily the largest and most stable life forms on Earth.

  • Fungi:

    • This kingdom includes fungi, such as molds, yeast, and mushrooms.

    • These organisms decompose organic matter (humus) and absorb nutrients from other organisms. They are eukaryotic and non-motile.

  • Animalia:

    • This kingdom includes all animals, which are multicellular and mostly motile.

    • These organisms depend on other organisms for nutrition and have various types of physical structures.


    • Whittaker's classification was an effective way to categorize the vast diversity of life and was used for many years in biology to understand the relationships between different organisms

    Which law of physics states that the force between the two electric charges reduces to aquarter of its former value when the distance between them is doubled?

        Pascal's Law

        Hooke's Law

        Stefan's Law

        Coulomb's Law

    Solution

    According to Coulomb's law, the force of attraction or repulsion between two charged bodies is directly proportional to the product of their charges and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them.

    The mathematical expression for Coulomb's law is F = kQ1Q2/r^2. 

    What the variables mean 

    • F: The force between the two charges, measured in Newtons
    • k: The Coulomb's Law constant, equal to 9 x 10^9 N m^2/C^2
    • Q1: The magnitude of the first charge, measured in Coulombs
    • Q2: The magnitude of the second charge, measured in Coulombs
    • r: The distance between the two charges, measured in meters
