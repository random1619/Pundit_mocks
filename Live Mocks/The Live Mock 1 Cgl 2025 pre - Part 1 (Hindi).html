English

The following sentence has been split into four segments. Identify the segment that contains a grammatical error.

Neither you

nor your friend

are guilty.    

No error

Solution

Replace ARE with IS.

CONCEPT:- "Neither....nor" is a correlative conjunction which is used to connect two SUBJECTS. The rules for subject-verb agreement with "neither...nor" are:

1. Singular Subjects : When both subjects connected by "neither...nor" are singular, the verb form is singular.
Example: "Neither he nor his friend is going to the party."

2. Plural Subjects : When both subjects connected by "neither...nor" are plural, the verb form is plural.
Example: "Neither the boys nor the girls are going to the party."

3. Mixed Subjects: When one subject is singular and the other is plural, the verb form is determined by the closest subject.
Example: "Neither the boys nor their sister IS going to the party."

Here, the singular subject "sister" is closer to the verb, so the verb form is singular.
So, the correct sentence would be - Neither You nor your friend IS guilty.
