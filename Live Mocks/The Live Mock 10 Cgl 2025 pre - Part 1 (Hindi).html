    Charle’s Law

    Gay Lusac’s Law

    Avogadro’s Law    

    Newton’s Law

Solution
Charles's Law is a fundamental principle in physics and thermodynamics. It states that the volume of a given mass of gas is directly proportional to its absolute temperature, provided the pressure remains constant. Mathematically, it is expressed as:
V₁ / T₁ = V₂ / T₂
Where:
V₁ and V₂ are the initial and final volumes of the gas.
T₁ and T₂ are the initial and final absolute temperatures [in Kelvin].
This law was formulated by Jacques Charles, a French physicist, and it demonstrates how gases expand when heated. It's an essential concept in understanding the behaviour of gases.

G = 6.67430 × 10⁻¹¹ N·m²/kg²


Newton's law of universal gravitation is a fundamental principle in physics proposed by Sir Isaac Newton. It states that every particle in the universe attracts every other particle with a force that is directly proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between their centers. Mathematically, it is expressed as:
F = G × [m₁ × m₂] / r²


Where: ‘F’ is the gravitational force between two masses, ‘G’ is the gravitational constant [6.67430 × 10⁻¹¹ N·m²/kg²], ‘m₁ and m₂’ are the masses of the two objects and ‘r’ is the distance between the centres of the two objects.
This law explains phenomena ranging from the falling of objects on Earth to the motion of planets around the Sun.
