An economy in which means of production are used in a manner such that social welfare is maximized is called:

Socialist Economy

Capitalist Economy

Mixed Economy    

None of these

Solution

Mixed Economy: A mixed economy is the one in which there is private as well as public ownership of the means of production. Production decisions are governed largely by the principle of profit maximisation, but not without checks and balances of social justice.


Socialist Economy: A socialist economy is the one in which there is a social (collective/public) ownership of the means of production, and economic decisions are taken by some central authority of the government with a view to maximise social welfare.


Capitalist Economy: A capitalist economy is defined as the one in which means of production are owned by the individuals, and the individuals are free to take their economic decisions, as guided by the principle of profit maximisation.
