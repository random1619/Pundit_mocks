Which of the following is/are key components of the National Health Stack proposed by the NITI Aayog?


1. National Health Electronic Registries
2. A Federated Personal Health Records (PHR) Framework
3. A new Indian Medical Association


Select the correct answer using the code given below:

Only 1 & 2

Only 2 & 3

Only 1 & 3    

All of the Above

Solution

The National Health Stack (NHS) proposed by the NITI Aayog aims to create a comprehensive digital framework to support the healthcare system in India. Here are explanations for the key components mentioned:


National Health Electronic Registries: These registries aim to create a digital database of all health records and related data, ensuring standardized and accurate information storage across the country. This helps in better management of health records and facilitates data-driven decision-making.


A Federated Personal Health Records (PHR) Framework: This framework focuses on enabling individuals to have their own digital health records that can be accessed and shared securely with healthcare providers. This ensures that patients have control over their health data and can provide accurate information to different healthcare facilities as needed.


A new Indian Medical Association: This is not part of the National Health Stack. The Indian Medical Association (IMA) is an existing professional body of doctors in India and is not related to the NHS proposed by the NITI Aayog.


Hence, the correct components are the National Health Electronic Registries and the Federated Personal Health Records (PHR) Framework, making the correct answer 1 and 2 only.

Solution

The environmental Kuznets curve (EKC) is a relationship between environmental quality and economic development.
According to this theory, many indicators of environmental degradation tend to get worse as modern economic growth occurs until average income reaches a certain point over the course of development.
In other words, the environmental Kuznets curve suggests that economic development initially leads to deterioration in the environment, but after a certain level of economic growth, a society begins to improve its relationship with the environment, and levels of environmental degradation reduce.
The EKC suggests that the solution to pollution is economic growth.
