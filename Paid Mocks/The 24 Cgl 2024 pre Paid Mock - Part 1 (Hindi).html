SOME vs ANY :- "Some" and "any" are both words that can be used with countable and uncountable nouns, but their usage depends on the context and the type of sentence:
SOME - Typically used in positive statements, offers, suggestions, and questions that expect an affirmative answer. For example,
"I bought some candles" or "Would you like some wine?"
"Some" can also imply a specific type or form, such as "Some of my Facebook friends live in San Diego".
ANY - Typically used in negative statements, questions that expect a negative answer, expressions of uncertainty, and questions that ask about an absence or lack. For example, "He does not have any friends on the team" or "Do we need any rice?"
"Any" can also describe an indefinite or incomplete quantity when the speaker doesn't want to specify an exact amount.
