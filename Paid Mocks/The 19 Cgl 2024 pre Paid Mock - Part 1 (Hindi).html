Which of the following is a method to measure the National Income?

Expenditure Method

Income Method

Production Method    

All of the above

Solution

National Income refers to the total value of all goods and services produced within a country’s borders in a given period of time (usually one year) and are used to measure the economic performance of a country.

Production Method: Calculates National Income by adding the value of all goods and services within a country.

National Income = Gross Value Added + Taxes – Subsidies

Income Method: Calculates national income by adding up all income received by the factors of production such as wages, profits and rent.

National Income = Wages + Interest + Profit + Rent

Expenditure Method: Calculates National Income by adding up the value added by all sectors of the economy.

National Income = Consumptions + Investments + Government Spending + Net Export
