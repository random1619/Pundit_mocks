The quality of petrol is expressed by:

Cetane number

Octane number

Butane number    

Reynolds number

Solution

The octane rating of a fuel (such as gasoline) indicates its resistance to knocking or pinging during combustion in a spark ignition engine. This rating compares the fuel's anti-knock properties to those of ISO-octane. The higher-octane number of a fuel means the better anti-knocking capacity for that fuel.

As the octane number is used to measure the ignition stability of gasoline (petrol), the cetane number is used as a quality parameter of diesel. The combustion of diesel without spark is provided by compression. Thus as soon as the diesel fuel is ignited and compressed the cetane number will be higher. This identifies the good quality of diesel.

The number is divisible by 9 if sum of all the digits of the Numbers is a multiple of 9.
Divisibility rule of 8:
The number is divisible by 8 if last three digits are divisible by 8 .

D. 953784 = 9+5+3+7+8+4 = 36, which is divisible by 9.
And 953784 is also divisible by 8.
Hence option D 953784 is divisible by 9 & 8 both.
Hence, 953784 is divisible by 72.
