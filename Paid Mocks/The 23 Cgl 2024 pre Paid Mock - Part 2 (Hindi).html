Inferior goods are those whose income effect is:

negative

positive

zero    

No effect

Solution

Inferior Goods: These are the goods, the demand for which decreases as income of buyers rises. There is inverse/negative relationship between income and demand. in case of inferior goods, income effect Is negative.

How Demand Curve of an Inferior Good changes when Income of the Buyers changes?

Here also, there are two situations as under:-

(i) Increase in Income: In a situation of increase in income, less of the (inferior) good is purchased. The consumer/buyer prefers to shift on to superior substitutes, because now he can afford them. Buying less of a commodity at its existing price implies a backward shift in demand curve or decrease in demand.

(ii) Decrease in Income: If income decreases, the consumer, already consuming an inferior good, is further compelled to depend on it. Maybe he has to further cut his consumption of superior substitute and buy more of the inferior goods. It implies a situation of forward shift in demand curve or increase in demand for inferior goods.
