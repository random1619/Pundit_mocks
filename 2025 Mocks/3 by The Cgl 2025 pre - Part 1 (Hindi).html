Which form of farming is called 'Slash and Burn' Agriculture?

Primitive subsistence farming

Commercial farming

Intensive subsistence farming    

Hydroponic farming

Solution

Primitive subsistence farming is called as slash and burn agriculture. Farmers clear a patch of land and produce cereals and other food crops to sustain their families. When the soil fertility decreases, the farmers shift and clear a fresh patch of land for cultivation.

Slash and burn farming is known by different names in various parts of the world.  Some of the common alternative names are:

1.     Swidden Agriculture: A common term used, especially in tropical regions.

2.     Shifting Cultivation: This term highlights the practice of moving from one plot of land to another after the soil loses its fertility.

3.     Jhum Farming: Used in Northeastern India, particularly in the states of Assam, Nagaland, and Mizoram.

4.     Milpa Farming: A term used in Central America, especially in Mexico, referring to the traditional slash-and-burn farming method.

5.     Kaingin Farming: Used in the Philippines to describe the slash-and-burn technique.
