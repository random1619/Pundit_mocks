Match the following:

Congress Session

President

A) 1905 [Banaras Session]

1) Annie Besant

B) 1917 [Calcutta Session]

2) Gopal Krishna Gokhale

C) 1924 [Belgaum Session]

3) JL Nehru

D) 1929 [Lahore Session]

4) MK Gandhi

A-2, B-1, C-4, D-3

A-3, B-4, C-1, D-2

A-2, B-3, C-4, D-1

A-1, B-2, C-3, D-4

Solution

1.1905 [Banaras Session]: Gopal Krishna Gokhale
The Banaras Session of 1905 was presided over by Gopal Krishna Gokhale, who was a prominent leader and social reformer in the early stages of the Indian independence movement.
2.1917 [Calcutta Session]: Annie Besant
In 1917, Annie Besant became the first woman to preside over the Indian National Congress at the Calcutta Session. Besant was an important figure in the Indian freedom movement and had a significant role in promoting self-rule for India. She was an ardent supporter of the Home Rule Movement, which advocated for India’s self-governance within the British Empire.
3.1924 [Belgaum Session]: Mahatma Gandhi [MK Gandhi]
The Belgaum Session of 1924 was presided over by Mahatma Gandhi, who played a central role in shaping the Indian independence struggle. This session took place after Gandhi had been released from jail, and his leadership was critical during the non-cooperation movement and other important events in the fight for independence.
4.1929 [Lahore Session]: Jawaharlal Nehru
The Lahore Session of 1929 was presided over by Jawaharlal Nehru, who became the Congress President during this session. It was a pivotal moment in the freedom struggle as it was at this session that the Purna Swaraj [Complete Independence] resolution was passed. Nehru’s leadership marked a significant shift in the Congress towards a more radical demand for full independence from British rule.
