    NaCl
    KCl
    BaCl2    
    CaCl2
Solution
Above given all option give a white precipitation with aqueous AgNO3 but they give different colours in Flame test.
Silver nitrate [AgNO3] reacts with halide ions [like Cl−] to form a white precipitate of silver halides [AgCl]. This reaction helps identify the presence of chloride ions in a solution.
Example reaction: Cl− + AgNO3 → AgCl [white precipitate] + NO3−
Flame tests are used to identify the presence of certain metal ions based on the color they produce when heated in a flame. Barium ions [Ba2+] produce a characteristic green flame, Sodium [yellow], Potassium [lilac], and Calcium [brick-red]. Conclusion: The compound that gives a white precipitate with aqueous AgNO3 and a green flame test is: BaCl2
